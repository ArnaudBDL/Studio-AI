# Music Studio – Glossary

This glossary defines the terminology used across this music studio repository.
Terms may differ from academic music theory and are adapted for AI-assisted music creation (e.g. Suno).

Version: 1.0
Last update: 2026-04-24



## Musical Structure

### Section
A distinct part of a track defined by harmony, rhythm, mood, or instrumentation.
Example: intro, verse, chorus, bridge, movement.

### Movement
A large autonomous musical section with its own identity.
Typically used in multi-part or progressive compositions.

### Linear Structure
Song structure that evolves continuously without returning to previous sections.

### Cyclic Structure
Structure based on repetition with variation (loops, verses/choruses).

### Slow Build
Gradual accumulation of texture, harmony, or intensity over time, without abrupt transitions.



## Harmony & Melody

### Static Harmony
Harmony that remains on the same chord or tonal center for an extended period.

### Harmonic Drift
Gradual, often subtle modulation without clear cadence.

### Modal Color
Use of modes (Dorian, Phrygian, etc.) to define emotional tone rather than functional harmony.

### Tension
Perceived instability created by harmony, melody, rhythm, or sound design.

### Resolution
Moment where tension decreases, often through harmonic or textural simplification.



## Rhythm & Tempo

### Pulse
Perceived rhythmic heartbeat, even in absence of drums.

### Free Tempo
Non-metrical or loosely timed sections.

### Minimal Percussion
Rhythmic elements present but not driving the composition.

### Groove-Less
Intentional absence of repeating rhythmic patterns.



## Lyrics & Narrative

### Fragmented Lyrics
Lyrics presented as disconnected phrases or images rather than a linear story.

### Internal Monologue
Lyrics written as thoughts, not addressed to a listener or character.

### Abstract Lyrics
Lyrics focusing on imagery and emotion rather than explicit meaning.

### Narrative Anchor
A recurring theme, image, or phrase that stabilizes abstract lyrics.



## Mood & Atmosphere

### Mood
Primary emotional intention of a track (melancholic, anxious, hopeful).

### Atmosphere
The sonic environment perceived by the listener (spacey, claustrophobic, vast).

### Dark
Low brightness, minor tonal centers, dense or distorted textures.

### Ethereal
Soft transients, long reverbs, high-frequency emphasis, low rhythmic definition.

### Cinematic
Wide stereo field, dynamic contrast, slow pacing, emotional progression.



## Production & Sound Design

### Texture
The sonic density and interaction between layers.

### Organic
Sounds perceived as acoustic, imperfect, or human.

### Synthetic
Sounds clearly electronic or processed.

### Sound Bed
Continuous background layer supporting the composition.

### Foreground Element
Element intentionally pushed forward in the mix.



## AI / Prompting Specific

### Prompt
Text description provided to the AI to generate music or lyrics.

### Style Prompt
Prompt segment describing genre, mood, instrumentation, and references.

### Lyrics Prompt
Prompt segment describing themes, tone, narrative voice.

### Prompt Leakage
When undesired stylistic elements appear due to vague or overloaded prompts.

### Over-Specification
Excessive detail reducing AI creativity or causing incoherence.

### Prompt Token Weight
Implicit importance given to early vs late prompt elements.



## Project & Workflow

### Brief
Initial creative intent and constraints of a project.

### Arbitrage
Documented decision between multiple creative options.

### Iteration
A generation or version produced after modifying parameters or prompts.

### Reference Track
Existing piece used for analysis or inspiration (not imitation).

### Prompt Final
Validated prompt considered ready for production or export.
